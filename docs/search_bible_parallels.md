# **Bible Parallels Search Guide**

This tool helps you discover and study parallel passages throughout Scripture—similar accounts, repeated teachings, and thematically related texts that illuminate each other when studied together.

## **What is Bible Parallels Search?**

The Bible Parallels Search tool provides access to collections of parallel passages organized by topic, event, or teaching. These curated sets help you compare how different biblical writers recorded the same events or taught similar truths.

## **What You'll Find**

For each parallel collection, the tool provides:
- **Collection Title**: Clear identification of the parallel set
- **Description**: What the parallels are about
- **Passage List**: All parallel texts grouped together
- **Context**: Why these passages are considered parallel
- **Comparison Notes**: Key similarities and differences
- **Bible References**: Direct links to each parallel passage

## **Types of Parallels**

### **1. Gospel Parallels (Synoptic Harmonies)**

Same events recorded in multiple Gospels:
- **Jesus' Birth**: Matthew 1-2, Luke 1-2
- **Temptation**: Matthew 4:1-11, Mark 1:12-13, Luke 4:1-13
- **Sermon on Mount/Plain**: Matthew 5-7, Luke 6:17-49
- **Feeding 5000**: All four Gospels
- **Triumphal Entry**: All four Gospels
- **Crucifixion**: All four Gospels
- **Resurrection**: All four Gospels

### **2. Old Testament Parallels**

Repeated historical accounts:
- **Samuel-Kings / Chronicles**: Same history, different perspectives
- **Repeated Psalms**: Psalm 14 / Psalm 53
- **Duplicate Prophecies**: Isaiah 2:2-4 / Micah 4:1-3
- **Law Repetitions**: Exodus 20 / Deuteronomy 5 (Ten Commandments)

### **3. Thematic Parallels**

Similar teachings or concepts:
- **Faith Examples**: Hebrews 11 parallels stories from OT
- **Love Chapters**: 1 Corinthians 13, 1 John 4
- **Creation Accounts**: Genesis 1, Genesis 2, Psalm 104
- **Last Days Teaching**: Matthew 24, Mark 13, Luke 21

### **4. Quotation Parallels**

OT quoted in NT:
- **Messianic Prophecies**: Isaiah 53 quoted in NT passion narratives
- **Psalm Quotations**: NT writers quoting Psalms about Jesus
- **Law Citations**: Jesus and apostles citing Torah

### **5. Typological Parallels**

OT types fulfilled in NT:
- **Passover / Lord's Supper**
- **Tabernacle / Christ's Body**
- **Manna / Bread of Life**
- **Bronze Serpent / Crucifixion**

## **Understanding Parallel Types**

### **Synoptic Parallels**

**What "Synoptic" Means:**
- "Seeing together"
- Matthew, Mark, Luke share much material
- John often different

**Why Parallels Exist:**
- Different audiences
- Different emphases
- Different details remembered
- Different theological purposes

**What to Look For:**
- Verbal similarities (same words)
- Structural similarities (same order)
- Unique additions
- Different emphases

### **Chronicles Parallels**

**Why Repetition:**
- Samuel-Kings: prophetic perspective
- Chronicles: priestly perspective
- Written for different audiences
- Different theological emphases

**Key Differences:**
- Chronicles omits some negative events
- Chronicles adds genealogies
- Chronicles emphasizes temple worship
- Chronicles focuses more on Judah

### **Psalm Parallels**

**Repeated Psalms:**
- Psalm 14 / 53 (almost identical)
- Psalm 70 / 40:13-17 (excerpt repeated)

**Why Repeated:**
- Different collections compiled
- Important themes restated
- Liturgical use in different settings
