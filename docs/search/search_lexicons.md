# **Lexicon Search Guide**

This tool provides access to Hebrew, Greek, and Aramaic lexicons for studying the original languages of Scripture. Look up biblical words to discover their meanings, nuances, and usage in their original languages.

## **What is Lexicon Search?**

The Lexicon Search tool gives you access to comprehensive lexicons (dictionaries of biblical languages) that define and explain Hebrew, Greek, and Aramaic words found in Scripture. Study the original languages to gain deeper understanding of biblical texts.

## **What You'll Find**

For each lexical entry, the tool provides:
- **Original Word**: Hebrew, Greek, or Aramaic term in original script
- **Transliteration**: How to pronounce it in English letters
- **Strong's Number**: Reference number for cross-referencing
- **Definition**: What the word means
- **Usage**: How it's used in Scripture
- **Semantic Range**: Full range of possible meanings
- **Etymology**: Word origins and linguistic roots
- **Grammatical Information**: Part of speech, forms
- **Related Words**: Cognates and derivatives
- **Biblical References**: Where the word appears in Scripture

## **How to Use**

1. **Enter a Word**: Type a Hebrew or Greek word, transliteration, or Strong's number
2. **Select from Suggestions**: Autocomplete shows matching terms as you type
3. **Press Enter**: Load the lexical entry
4. **Read Definition**: Study the word's meaning and usage
5. **Check References**: See where the word appears in Scripture
6. **Explore Related Words**: Follow links to connected terms
7. **Compare Translations**: Note how different versions translate it

### **Search Features**

## **Languages Covered**

### **Biblical Hebrew**

**Old Testament Language:**
- **Script**: Hebrew alphabet (22 letters)
- **Reading**: Right to left
- **Characteristics**:
  - Consonantal script (vowels added later)
  - Root system (typically 3 consonants)
  - Rich metaphorical vocabulary
- **Examples**:
  - אֱלֹהִים (*Elohim*) - God
  - שָׁלוֹם (*Shalom*) - Peace
  - חֶסֶד (*Chesed*) - Steadfast love

### **Biblical Greek (Koine)**

**New Testament Language:**
- **Script**: Greek alphabet (24 letters)
- **Period**: Common Greek (300 BC - 300 AD)
- **Characteristics**:
  - Precise tense system
  - Rich vocabulary
  - Philosophical terminology
- **Examples**:
  - ἀγάπη (*Agape*) - Love
  - λόγος (*Logos*) - Word
  - χάρις (*Charis*) - Grace

### **Biblical Aramaic**

**Limited Biblical Use:**
- **Script**: Similar to Hebrew
- **Usage**: Parts of Daniel and Ezra
- **Characteristics**:
  - Related to Hebrew
  - Language of Jesus
  - International language of ancient Near East

## **Example Workflows**

### **Word Study - Agape (Love)**

1. Search: `Agape` or `G26`
2. Read lexicon entry:
   - **Greek**: ἀγάπη
   - **Transliteration**: agapē
   - **Strong's**: G26
   - **Part of Speech**: Noun, feminine
   - **Definition**: Love, especially of God for humanity and Christians for each other
   - **Distinction**: Different from:
     - *Eros* (romantic love - not in NT)
     - *Phileo* (brotherly love, friendship)
     - *Storge* (family affection - not in NT)
3. Note theological significance:
   - Self-sacrificial love
   - God's nature (1 John 4:8)
   - Greatest commandment (Mark 12:30-31)
   - Defining Christian characteristic (John 13:35)
4. Study key passages:
   - John 3:16
   - Romans 5:8
   - 1 Corinthians 13
   - 1 John 4:7-21
5. Compare with *phileo*:
   - Search `G5368` (phileo)
   - Note John 21:15-17 (Jesus and Peter)
   - Understand distinction matters
6. Document insights about biblical love

### **Word Study - Hesed (Steadfast Love)**

1. Search: `Hesed` or `H2617`
2. Read lexicon entry:
   - **Hebrew**: חֶסֶד
   - **Transliteration**: chesed
   - **Strong's**: H2617
   - **Definition**: Steadfast love, lovingkindness, mercy, faithfulness, covenant loyalty
   - **Rich Meaning**: No single English word captures it
3. Note covenant context:
   - Loyal love within relationship
   - Faithful to commitments
   - Both God's character and expected human behavior
4. Translations vary:
   - KJV: "mercy," "lovingkindness"
   - ESV: "steadfast love"
   - NIV: "love," "unfailing love"
   - All trying to capture rich meaning
5. Study key passages:
   - Exodus 34:6 (God's self-revelation)
   - Psalm 136 (refrain: "His hesed endures forever")
   - Psalm 89:1-2 (hesed of the LORD)
   - Hosea (hesed in marriage metaphor)
6. Appreciate untranslatable richness
7. See God's covenant faithfulness

### **Word Study - Logos (Word)**

1. Search: `Logos` or `G3056`
2. Read lexicon entry:
   - **Greek**: λόγος
   - **Transliteration**: logos
   - **Strong's**: G3056
   - **Meanings**:
     - Word, speech, saying
     - Account, reason, motive
     - Divine utterance
     - Christ as the Word
3. Note semantic range:
   - Everyday speech
   - Reasoned discourse
   - Philosophical concept (Greek philosophy)
   - Theological term (John 1:1)
4. Compare with *rhema* (G4487):
   - Logos: general word, message, principle
   - Rhema: specific utterance, spoken word
5. Study John's usage:
   - John 1:1 - "In beginning was the Word"
   - Combines Hebrew concept (God's word creates)
   - And Greek philosophy (divine reason/wisdom)
   - Identifies Jesus as God's ultimate communication
6. Trace through John 1:1-18
7. See profound Christology

### **Comparing Synonyms - Peace**

1. Hebrew peace:
   - Search: `Shalom` (H7965)
   - **Meaning**: Peace, completeness, welfare, health
   - **Rich concept**: Not just absence of war
   - More than tranquility
   - Wholeness, well-being, flourishing
2. Greek peace:
   - Search: `Eirene` (G1515)
   - **Meaning**: Peace, harmony, rest
   - **NT Usage**: Often translating Hebrew shalom
   - Jesus as source of peace (John 14:27)
   - Peace with God (Romans 5:1)
3. Compare concepts:
   - Both transcend English "peace"
   - Relational wholeness
   - Spiritual wellbeing
   - Eschatological hope
4. Document comprehensive understanding

## **Understanding Lexical Information**

### **Strong's Numbers**

**Numbering System:**
- **H####**: Hebrew/Aramaic words (H1-H8674)
- **G####**: Greek words (G1-G5624)
- **Standard Reference**: Universally recognized
- **Cross-Tool**: Used across Bible software

**Benefits:**
- Find word even if can't spell/type it
- Consistent across translations
- Links concordance to lexicon
- Research tool

### **Transliteration**

**Purpose:**
- Allows English speakers to reference original words
- Pronunciation guide
- No need to learn original scripts (though helpful)

**Limitations:**
- Not always standardized
- Doesn't capture all sounds precisely
- Vowel pronunciation debated (especially Hebrew)

### **Etymology**

**Word Roots:**
- Hebrew: Usually 3-consonant root
- Greek: Various formations
- Understanding roots illuminates meaning

**Example:**
- **Hebrew**: שׁ-ל-ם (sh-l-m) root → various words about completion/peace
- **Greek**: λογ- root → logos, logic, logical, etc.

### **Semantic Range**

**Why It Matters:**
- Single word may have multiple meanings
- Context determines which sense applies
- Translators must choose
- Understanding range helps interpretation

**Example - Greek *Sarx* (Flesh):**
- Physical body
- Human nature
- Sinful nature
- Human perspective
- Context determines which meaning
