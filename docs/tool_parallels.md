# **Bible Parallels Guide**

This tool displays Gospel parallels, harmonies, and related passages that describe the same or similar events across different books of the Bible.

## **What are Bible Parallels?**

Bible Parallels help you study events that are recorded in multiple books of the Bible, particularly in the Gospels. This tool organizes parallel accounts into thematic collections, making it easy to compare how different authors describe the same event.

## **What You'll Find**

The tool includes major collections of parallel passages:

### **Gospel Harmonies**

Parallel accounts from the four Gospels:
- **Synoptic Parallels**: Events recorded in Matthew, Mark, and Luke
- **John's Gospel**: Events unique to or shared with John's Gospel
- **Birth Narratives**: Parallel accounts of Jesus' birth
- **Passion Narratives**: Parallel accounts of Jesus' death and resurrection

### **Old Testament Parallels**

- **Kings and Chronicles**: Parallel accounts of Israel's history
- **Samuel and Chronicles**: Overlapping narratives
- **Exodus through Deuteronomy**: Repeated laws and events

### **Historical Harmonies**

Collections showing:
- How different authors describe the same event
- Chronological relationships between accounts
- Unique details in each version

## **How to Use Bible Parallels**

1. **Open the Tool**: Select "Parallels" from the tools menu.
2. **Browse Collections**: Scroll through the list of parallel collections.
3. **Expand a Collection**: Click on any collection to see its contents.
4. **View References**: Click Bible references to open those passages.
5. **Compare Accounts**: Read parallel passages side by side.

## **Tips for Effective Use**

1. **Compare Carefully**: Read all parallel accounts before drawing conclusions.
2. **Note Unique Details**: Each account may include information the others omit.
3. **Context Matters**: Open full chapters to see each parallel in its broader context.
4. **Track Differences**: Note variations in wording, order, or emphasis.
5. **Harmonize**: Consider how accounts complement rather than contradict each other.
6. **Study Authors**: Learn each author's style and theological emphasis.
7. **Use with Commentary**: Consult commentaries for scholarly insights on parallels.

## **Common Parallel Types**

### **1. Identical Core Events**

Same event, similar details:
- Feeding of the 5,000
- Healing of the blind man
- Cleansing the temple

### **2. Similar but Distinct Events**

Possibly different occasions:
- Two temple cleansings (John 2 vs. Synoptics)
- Multiple healing accounts
- Repeated teachings in different contexts

### **3. Complementary Accounts**

Different details, same event:
- Birth narratives (Matthew vs. Luke)
- Resurrection appearances (each Gospel adds details)
- Ascension accounts (Luke, Acts)

## **Historical Context**

Understanding parallel accounts requires awareness of:
- **Ancient Biography**: Different standards than modern history
- **Oral Tradition**: Accounts passed down before written
- **Thematic Arrangement**: Not always strictly chronological
- **Author Purpose**: Each writer had specific goals and audiences
